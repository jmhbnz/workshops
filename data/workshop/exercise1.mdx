Welcome to the *OpenShift automation workshop*.

In this workshop you'll explore how platform engineers can use [Ansible Automation Platform](https://www.redhat.com/en/technologies/management/ansible) to automate common operational tasks in [OpenShift](https://www.redhat.com/en/technologies/cloud-computing/openshift) with re-usable, composable playbooks assembled in to end to end workflows.

Additionally this workshop will provide some foundational knowledge and experience of working with Ansible Automation Platform so OpenShift platform engineers will be prepared to support ansible users within their organisation.

**Let's get started!**


## 1.1 - The scenario

For this hands on workshop, we're going to be an OpenShift team member at **ACME Financial Services**, a large bank operating in New Zealand. They run a significant number of customer facing workloads on OpenShift and have a small core team of platform engineers supporting the platform.

![acme](/workshops/static/images/fsi-security/acme.jpg)

Throughout this workshop we'll be in the shoes of Sarah, a senior OpenShift Platform Engineer at ACME, a day in the life for her typically sees her handling platform health and maintenance tasks, assisting application teams with feature requests and she is also rostered on to the platform incident management queue on a regular rotation.

![acme](/workshops/static/images/fsi-security/sarah-desk.png)


## 1.2 - Understanding the environment

For this workshop you'll be given access to the ACME Financial Services Production OpenShift `4.21` cluster and Ansible Automation Platform `2.7`.


![cluster](/workshops/static/images/fsi-security/acme-cluster.png)
 *ACME Financial Services OpenShift Web Console*



## 1.3 - Obtain your environment

To get underway open your web browser and navigate to [https://catalog.demo.redhat.com/workshop/cjjhdp](https://catalog.demo.redhat.com/workshop/cjjhdp) to allocate yourself a workshop environment.

Once the page loads you can login with any email address and the password provided by your workshop facilitator.


![Workshop login](/workshops/static/images/ocp-aap/workshop-login.png)
 *Login to obtain a workshop environment*


After logging in you will be presented with access details for OpenShift and Ansible Automation Platform. Use credentials on that page to log in to the OpenShift Web Console now.

If you have an environment and are logged in to the OpenShift Web Console, you're now ready to move on to [Exercise 2](/workshop/exercise2), let the challenge begin! 🎉
